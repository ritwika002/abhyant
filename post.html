Ancient Indian Marriage Culture

Exploring the timeless traditions and rich rituals of ancient Indian marriage culture.

Posted by Team Abhyant on July 14, 2024

Indian weddings are a tapestry of traditions, colours, and rituals that date back centuries, encapsulating the rich cultural diversity of the subcontinent. From vibrant attire to sumptuous feasts, each wedding is a unique amalgamation of sacred rituals, joyful festivities, and heartfelt hospitality, making it a spectacle that resonates with both tradition and modernity.

The Vibrancy of the Indian Wedding Season

It is a time when friends and family convene to commemorate the unification of two people who are in love. It is not only the wedding pair that gets married, but the entire family. Indian culture has affluent rituals and traditions. A wedding is one the most important events in an individual’s life which signifies a great festival of devotion, affection and values. Join us as we unravel the enchanting world of Indian weddings, where every ritual tells a story and every moment is a celebration of love, heritage, and togetherness.

The Liveliness of an Indian Wedding

An Indian Wedding is a phase marked by magnificence and cheerfulness, resonating with India’s opulent cultural heritage. The wedding season witnesses an unmatched scale of commemoration with almost 35 lakh weddings observed between the month of November to December. One must be aware of Indians’ fondness of colours and the weddings are a perfect demonstration of it. People from the western world are used to White Weddings but upon encountering an Indian Wedding they realize how people dressed in attires of varied colours still harmonize perfectly. Let alone the colour shades, some shades don’t even have a name of its own. Yet they all look amazing. Now coming to the ornaments, you’d have your eyes fixed to it since there is a decorative item for every part of the body, i.e. arms, feet, waist, ears, neck, head and forehead. The beauty of these ornaments is a treat for your eyes!

The Financial Influence of the Indian Wedding Season

An Indian Wedding is not just a cultural festival but also a very strong monetary drive. The monetary implications are worth noticing with Delhi alone witnessing for over 3.5 lakh weddings, enhancing the business landscape significantly. The Confederation of All India Traders estimates a staggering ₹4.25 lakh crore in income, underscoring the Indian Wedding Season’s role in augmenting diverse sectors, no matter the challenges of inflation. Commonly, the bride’s side is the one to provide with all the necessary supplies for the wedding and even pays for every cost associated with it. Dowry had been a prevalent practice in India's modern era and in this context, it can be in the form of a payment of cash or gifts from the bride's family to the bridegroom's family upon marriage. Their families have to ensure that everything is going well and they would just do everything to make it a success.

Customs and Rituals at Indian Wedding Receptions

Indian weddings are synonymous with celebration and happiness, marked by the outpouring of affection from family and friends towards the bride and groom. The customs observed during Indian wedding receptions vary across regions, yet they share common elements. In many parts of India, the tradition of "Kanya Daan" symbolizes the bride being given away and typically involves her not seeing the groom before the ceremony.

In Northern India, the bride participates in a traditional puja before entering her new household, while Southern Indian customs include an elaborate reception called "Kalasam" to welcome the bride. Additionally, there's a ritual where the groom's sister presents a sari to the bride as part of the ceremony. Eastern Indian weddings feature the lively "bouquet-throwing" ritual, where unmarried women attempt to catch bouquets thrown by their father or brother, expressing their desire to marry soon.

A Never-Ending Culinary Spread

There's no denying the love Indians have for cooking and enjoying elaborate, aromatic dishes. And what better occasion to indulge in these delicacies than during weddings? Hosts showcase the best regional favourites, offering guests an unparalleled culinary experience. Food is not just an integral part of Indian weddings; it's also a topic of lively post-wedding discussions among attendees. From a variety of savoury starters (both vegetarian and non-vegetarian) to an extensive main course featuring upwards of 50 different dishes, and a plethora of desserts, attending an Indian wedding is akin to embarking on a gastronomic journey.

Native Encounter

Indian weddings not only showcase the rich cultural tapestry of the country but also provide a deep dive into its diverse religious practices. From regional cuisine preferences to popular dance forms, attending an Indian wedding offers a comprehensive learning experience without needing to travel extensively. Whether you're relaxing at home or seated on a mat, each wedding unfolds as a treasure trove of cultural richness, making it an enriching and joyful occasion.
